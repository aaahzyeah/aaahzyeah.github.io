Sensair: A Tangible Tool Through Data-enabled Approach to Improve People's Perception of Indoor Air Quality

Product Design, Data-Enabled Design

Good air quality is essential for long-term health. And as stated by the World Health Organisation, even seemingly imperceptible levels of air pollution can cause premature deatH. The air quality (AQ) perception doesn’t always align with the actual AQ. Various air quality sensors were used to gain insight into the users day to day experience, related to air quality. A data tracking probe was formed and used in combination with a diary, and insights. This process resulted in a concept that measures in-door Dust and Gas concentration and visualizes it subtly, enabling the user to actively influence the indoor air quality (IAQ) in a positive way.

Date: 04/2020 --> 06/2020 (3 months)

Methodology

We used the Data-Enabled Design approach, which has an emphasis on using data as creative input. The method consisted of two main parts. The Contextual Step aims to gain an understanding of the design space, with all of its richness and complexities. The Informed Step aims to explore and advance the design, while situated in everyday life.

Data as Design Material

Objective Data (air quality) was collected by data probes in the contextual step, and by the prototype in the informed step. The subjective data was collected by the diary in both steps. We visualized the data together and discuess it with the participants to gain more insights about users. We also use the data to see how did the user experience the prototype in real situation, such as the using frequency, and time of using.

On the left side, there are two plots of the data visualization by Tabluea.

Interaction Flow

When people receive the notification from the prototype, they can touch the light conductive fibers to stop the vibration, after that, when they do any action with intentions to change the IAQ, they touch the fiber again. The second touch will trigger a rainbow light for 10 seconds, in this way, it can encourage users to do an action and give feedback to the system. The second touch is interpreted by the system that users are active with improving IAQ. In the coming 15 minutes, the system will keep monitoring the IAQ and informing users only by the light without vibration.
