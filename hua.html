Hua: An Organizational Culture Mapping Tool

Social Innovation, Service Design

Hua is a tangible tool that visualizes an organization's culture, based on the six cultural dimensions as described by Geert Hofstede. The goal was to create a tool or service for the Brabant Outcome Fund (BOF) through a transformative design approach. Using the tool can help create expectancy patterns and maintain work relationships. The design process was started with an embodied sketching session exploring, making and trying out different ideas. The context of the chosen concept was deconstructed to reframe the challenge. Afterward, Hua has developed into a robust physical prototype and a digital concept giving guidance after mapping the culture. The design was presented in an exhibition that showed Hua to the BOF and those who took an interest.

How It Works

Hua means flower in Chinese. This is a tool for visualizing your organizational culture.

The flower consists of twelve petals and a compass. Each petal has a cultural attribute on it, and each two opposite petals form one dimension, based on Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions Theory.

The 3 steps to blossom your culture flower are shown to the right.

And Then?

Scan it and get a digital version. If you digitalized your culture flower, archive it in the garden. Then you can:

Overlap them to see the similarities and differences in your organizational culture.

Click on a petal to get insights.

The garden keeper (may be the head of the organization or multi-cultural group leader), finds the statistics of all the culture flowers.

Context

BOF assembles a team from different organizations (e.g. the province, investors) for the entrepreneur. These team members have different ways of communicating and have different cultural backgrounds. They can use the cultural flower to visualize their cultural differences in order to gain more insight into how other organizations work and how they can deal with the differences. During or at the end of the collaboration, this can be used as a reflective tool.

BOF is maintaining relationships with different companies and institutions. Since all these different companies have different organizational cultures, BOF might benefit from sending a representative that has knowledge of these institutions and has similar cultural values or sent a colleague from BOF (with different cultural values) to prevent staying in a bubble. Representatives map their expected organizational culture, and with this, they are matched with different companies.

Another context that might be useful on macro level is to let companies map their culture which could help with setting up the team for the en- trepreneur on micro level.
