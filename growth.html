Development in Expertise Areas

Design and Research Process

With my bachelor's background in industrial design at ZJU, I have developed a good command of the user-centered design process and user research skillset. During my master's program at TU/e, I broadened my view to more design and research methodologies and learned that design and research are so tightly related.

In the core course "Constructive Design Research", I learned that we not only could use research to guide and value design, but also develop the approach towards Research through Design. The three approaches to conducting the research, namely the lab, field, and showroom, equipped me with an overall knowledge of how to plan and apply these approaches to guide the research process. After practice in "Project 2 Design Research", as well as the research internship in CDI, I learned how to frame the research question in design scope, and how to flexibly position research and design in my project based on my need.

Other design methodology, such as "Data-Enabled Design", and "Transformative Practices” that I learned from electives, enriched my library. Currently, I can choose to adopt those methodologies in projects based on the context and business need.
